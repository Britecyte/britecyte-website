Our platform

Solving adipose
immunogenicity.

Current adipose uses are limited to autologous tissue — patient and donor must be the same person. Britecyte has resolved adipose tissue immunogenicity, allowing allogeneic adipose (patient and donor are different persons) without rejection.

  1. 01

    Qualified donor adipose

    Human adipose tissue is recovered from qualified donors under established tissue-banking standards. This provides the biological starting material — native fat matrix with its extracellular structure intact — before any Britecyte processing begins.

  2. 02

    Immunogenicity resolution

    Britecyte's proprietary, patent-protected process addresses the immunogenicity that has historically restricted adipose to autologous use. The tissue is treated to enable allogeneic transplantation while preserving native architecture — not reduced to a synthetic gel or cross-linked filler.

  3. 03

    Native allograft

    The result is a ready-to-use adipose allograft developed without synthetic agents, cross-linkers, or foreign scaffolds. Lipoderma and Liposana maintain like-for-like tissue structure — biocompatible, off-the-shelf products physicians can implant directly.

  4. 04

    Patient restoration

    Trained physicians implant the allograft where adipose has been lost or is deficient — restoring the fat that supports healthy skin (Lipoderma) or providing structural cushioning for tissue repair in areas of adipose deficits (Liposana). Over 650 patients have received Britecyte allografts to date.

Our Story

Britecyte was born in 2020 out of a quiet laboratory, a global shutdown, and an unfinished journey.

When the COVID-19 pandemic forced the world to a standstill, Founder and Chief Scientific Officer Dr. Alla Danilkovitch saw an unexpected opportunity. Renting a single laboratory bench, she channeled the global silence into absolute, singular focus. Her mission? To solve complex, unmet medical needs through biocompatible, allogeneic human cell and tissue-based therapies.

By 2022, that intense isolation and time spent in the lab yielded a breakthrough: the successful development of Britecyte’s proprietary allogeneic adipose technology. With initial funding secured, Samson Tom joined as President and CEO to help build a team and transform this scientific breakthrough into a commercial reality.

But Britecyte’s roots run much deeper than 2020. For decades, the promise of regenerative medicine has captivated scientists. Alla, Sam, and early colleagues spent years at Osiris Therapeutics, helping develop and launch first-in-class products like Osteocel®, Cartiform®, Grafix®, and Stravix®. Their team also led the development of Prochymal® (later Ryoncil®), the world’s first approved stem cell therapy.

Today, Britecyte carries that 30-year legacy forward—focused on restoring and repairing adipose tissue where it is lost or dysfunctional.

News & updates

Latest from Britecyte.

January 2025

IND cleared for knee osteoarthritis trial

Britecyte receives FDA clearance to initiate Phase 1/2a study evaluating adipose allograft therapy for knee osteoarthritis.

The study will assess safety and preliminary efficacy in patients with symptomatic knee OA, building on Britecyte's existing clinical experience across more than 650 adipose allograft implantations. Enrollment is expected to begin at select U.S. sites in the coming quarters.

Clinical
June 2024

Lipoderma commercial launch

Britecyte introduces Lipoderma — the first complete adipose tissue allograft for aesthetic volume restoration through select trained physicians.

Launch begins with a curated network of physicians trained directly on the product, prioritizing like-for-like facial and hand restoration over synthetic augmentation. Early adoption has focused on patients experiencing age-related volume loss and GLP-1-associated facial thinning.
